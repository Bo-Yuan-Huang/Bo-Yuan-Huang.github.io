Security Research, Intel

I am an Offensive Security Researcher at Intel Corp. My primary research interests are in design automation and functional and security verification of computer systems, with a focus on formal methods and heterogeneous platforms. A central theme of my work is empowering designers with methodologies and frameworks to ensure systems' correctness and security guarantees by leveraging modeling and verification techniques such as model checking, fuzzing, and simulation. Recently, I have been exploring the role of formal-powered static analysis for security evaluation in both firmware and hardware CI/CD practices and opportunities for at-scale security vulnerability detection using machine learning. Before joining Intel, I received my B.S. from National Taiwan University and my M.A. and Ph.D. from Princeton University.
